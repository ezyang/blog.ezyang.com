Design of a programming language, but for programmers. Insights from 'Insane Asylum'

Tension of what is easy to implement and what is easy to use Thesis of book: if you change the design process, you can make these coincide Dancing bear metaphor Cognitive friction It's simply not enough to code it up, and add "an interface". Interface is fundamental to how software is put together Iteration and the myth of the unpredictable market Prototyping is expensive How (Email|Scheduling|Calendar) programs fail Software forgets (we learn about software, why not vice versa), is lazy, is parsimonious with information (doesn't say what's happening. from the perspective the user cares about), is inflexible, blames users (crash), won't take responsibility (dialog boxes) Simplicity v Control, Success v Understanding, Possible v Probable (Security?) Persona (a "cognitive handle" on the target audience) Tasks != Goals Polite software is interested in me, deferential, forthcoming, has common sense, anticipates my needs, responsive, taciturn about its personal problems, well informed, perceptive, self-confident, focused, fudgable, gives instant gratification, is trustworthy Advocacy for design documents
