If I asked you to describe what same-origin policy was, what might you say? At a first approximation, you might suggest, “scripts on one origin should not be able to access or modify data or scripts on another origin.” Of course, anyone who has ever worked on any web application can immediately call to mind multiple exceptions to this policy: tracking image tags, JSONP, CORS you name it. Same-origin policy is not so much a policy, as a suggestion. This is a problem: the incoherence of same-origin policy is killing web application security.
