- When debugging, approach the problem with an open mind. Make a large number of observations under varying circumstances free from any preconceptions about the nature of the problem. From your observations, generalize them into a hypothesis about the nature of the problem.
- 

In this essay I claim that the process of debugging a program mirrors that of science. I explain the inductivist and falsificationist views on science, how they apply to debugging practice, and then argue that more experienced developers debug in a more sophisticated style than these idealized notions.
